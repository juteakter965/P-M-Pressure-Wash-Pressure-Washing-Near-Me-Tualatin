Is pressure washing safe for all surfaces?

Not all surfaces should be cleaned with high pressure. That's why P&M Pressure Wash uses both pressure washing and soft washing techniques. Soft washing uses low pressure (under 500 PSI) with specialized cleaning solutions for delicate surfaces like wood siding, roofs, and painted surfaces. High-pressure washing (2,000-3,000 PSI) is appropriate for concrete, brick, and stone. We assess each surface and use the appropriate method to ensure safe, effective cleaning without damage.

Can you pressure wash in winter in Tualatin?

Yes, pressure washing can be performed year-round in Tualatin's mild climate, though spring and summer are most popular. We avoid washing when temperatures drop below 40¬∞F or during heavy rain. Winter washing is actually beneficial for removing moss and algae that thrive during wet months. However, many homeowners prefer spring cleaning after winter rains subside and before outdoor entertaining season begins.

No, you don't need to be home as long as we have access to the areas being cleaned and a water source. Many customers provide gate codes or leave gates unlocked and go about their day. Will pressure washing damage my landscaping?

When performed correctly by professionals, pressure washing should not harm landscaping. P&M Pressure Wash takes precautions to protect your plants including pre-wetting vegetation, using plant-safe cleaning solutions, covering delicate plants when necessary, and thoroughly rinsing all areas after cleaning. We're experienced in working around gardens, shrubs, and flower beds. If you have particularly sensitive plants, let us know and we'll take extra care.

How long does pressure washing take?

What's the difference between pressure washing and power washing?

Pressure washing and power washing are often used interchangeably, but technically power washing uses heated water while pressure washing uses cold water. For most residential applications in Tualatin, cold water pressure washing combined with appropriate cleaning solutions is perfectly effective. Heated water (power washing) is sometimes used for heavy oil or grease removal in commercial settings. P&M Pressure Wash uses the most effective method for each specific cleaning challenge.
